Bougatsa



Description


Bougatsa,is a Greek breakfast food (sweet or savoury).
Bougatsa has several versions with their own filling,
with the most popular the bougatsa krema (bougatsa cream)
that has semolina custard filling used as a sweet food and dessert.

The name comes from the Byzantine Greek πογάτσα (pogátsa),
from the ancient Roman pānis focācius, literally 'hearth bread';
comparable with the Italian "focaccia".

Ingredients

For the custard

  • 750 g whole milk
  • 180 g granulated sugar
  • 75 g semolina, fine
  • 1 tablespoon(s) vanilla extract
  • lemon zest, of 1 lemon
  • 45 g butter, cold
  • 3 egg yolks, of medium eggs

For the phyllo

  • 450 g hard flour
  • 10 g salt
  • 220 g water, at room temperature
  • 50 g butter, melted
  • 450 g butter, melted

To serve

  • icing sugar
  • cinnamon

Steps

For the custard

  1. In a pot, add the milk and sugar, and transfer over medium heat until the milk comes to a boil.
  2. Add the semolina and stir with a hand whisk, until the cream thickens.
  3. Remove from the heat and add the vanilla extract, lemon zest, cold butter, and mix until the butter melts.
  4. Add the yolks, salt, and mix.
  5. Transfer the custard into a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and place it into the refrigerator to cool well. Make sure that the plastic wrap touches the surface of the custard, so it does not make a crust while cooling.

For the phyllo

  1. In a mixer's bowl, add the flour, salt, water, and 50 g of the melted butter.
  2. Beat with the paddle attachment for 5-7 minutes at high speed, until there is an elastic dough.
  3. Remove from the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for 20 minutes.
  4. Remove the plastic wrap and cut into 4 pieces. Shape into balls and place them into a bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and let the dough rest for 20 more minutes.
  5. Take two of the dough balls and roll them out into small discs. Spread them onto a baking pan, cover with plastic wrap, and let them rest for 20 minutes.

To assemble

  1. Preheat the oven to 170° C (338° F) set to fan.
  2. Spread your working surface with melted butter and place the first dough. Roll it out softly with a rolling pin, not from the center but from its sides.
  3. Then, carefully spread the sides of the dough all around with your hands, by pulling the corners of the dough.
  4. Follow the same process, until you have a very thin phyllo.
  5. Spread ¼ of the butter and cut strips around the phyllo in order to form a parallelogram. Place the strips in the center of the phyllo.
  6. Add half of the filling in the center and close it like a folder. Set aside.
  7. Roll out the second dough in the same way, and spread ¼ of the melted butter.
  8. Place the bougatsa in the center of the phyllo, with the folded side facing down. Roll softly with the rolling pin, and fold the outer phyllo like a folder.
  9. Transfer the bougatsa, with the folded side facing down, into a baking pan lined with parchment paper and bake for 40-50 minutes.
  10. Follow the above process for the other two doughs as well. You can bake the second bougatsa too, or you can put it in the freezer.

To serve

  1. Cut the bougatsa into small pieces, dust with icing sugar, cinnamon, and serve.
